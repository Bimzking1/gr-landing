*Rule / reports from AI:
- For each of the 6 quiz steps, sum the points every chosen option contributes to each archetype (an option can feed more than one archetype). The archetype with the single highest total wins.
- Tie-break rule (for determinism): if two or more archetypes are exactly tied for the highest score, the one that comes first alphabetically by title wins. The same 6 answers always score the same totals and always resolve to the same tie-break, so the same inputs always produce the same result.
- 'Legendary signature bonus' (1.3 pts): each of the 8 Legendary archetypes has one 'signature' option in every step worth 1.3 points. Getting one or two of these alone won't win — but choosing the matching signature option in most/all 6 steps stacks enough points to beat the common/uncommon totals, which is exactly why Legendary archetypes stay rare (only reachable via a fairly specific, consistent combo).
- This was validated by brute-force simulation of all 6×6×5×6×6×6 = 38,880 possible input combinations (see 'Scoring Validation' sheet) to confirm the resulting win-rate per archetype roughly matches its target rarity-tier probability, and that every one of the 21 archetypes is actually reachable.
- All 38,880 possible answer combinations (6 jerseys × 6 shoes × 5 gear × 6 times × 6 companions × 6 goals) were enumerated and scored using the exact point tables in the 'Scoring System' sheet, with the same deterministic tie-break rule (alphabetical on exact ties).
- Result: all 21 archetypes are reachable (none score zero wins). Common/Uncommon/Epic/Legendary tier totals land within ~1 point of their target ranges; Rare lands about 1 point above its target range (20.84% vs. 15-20%) -- close enough to be a reasonable, defensible approximation without further tuning.
- Within-tier spread is uneven (e.g. Mood Booster Runner wins far more often than Weight Loss Runner, even though both are 'Common') because the source docs only ever gave a probability for the tier as a whole, never per archetype -- so no per-archetype target existed to tune against.
